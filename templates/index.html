Arauco Mexico

Arauco is one of the world's leading forestry and wood-panel companies, headquartered in Chile with operations in more than 80 countries. In Mexico it runs three production plants in Chihuahua, Durango, and Zitácuaro (Michoacán), manufacturing MDF boards, PB/MDP and melamine panels. The Durango site also produces urea-formaldehyde (UF) resin — the primary adhesive for all panel products.

USD/MXN — Historical & Forecast
The spot rate is the primary driver of Arauco's raw material costs. The orange line shows realized data through May-26; blue (consensus) and dark orange (pessimistic) lines represent analyst projections to Dec-26. A move from 17.34 → 20.50 (bearish scenario) would add ~MXN 60M to the 6-month cost base.
Inflation: Mexico vs. U.S. (%)
Banxico's target is 3% ±1 pp. MX inflation remains above the ceiling (4.53%) — this constrains rate cuts and helps sustain the Banxico–Fed carry spread. If US inflation re-accelerates, the Fed pauses cuts, strengthening the dollar and pressuring MXN.
Policy Rates (%)
The Banxico–Fed spread determines carry trade attractiveness. At its 2023 peak (7.6 pp), foreign investors poured capital into MXN-denominated assets, keeping the peso strong. Today the spread is only 2.875 pp — the carry trade unwind is a key bearish risk for the peso.
GDP Growth (%)
Mexico contracted –0.8% in Q1-2026 amid weak consumption and USMCA uncertainty, raising recession risk. Slower growth reduces FDI inflows → less peso demand → depreciation pressure. Banxico revised 2026 growth to 1.0–1.2%. Bars in blue represent the U.S., which maintains moderate expansion.
